Rock'n'roll or rock is a musical genre that appeared in the 1950s in the United States. His influences come from blues, jazz and country music. Having become popular at the end of the 1950s, rock then evolved to give birth to other styles, such as hard rock. The expression Rock'n'roll means "rock and turn" in English. It represents rock rhythm. Rock'n'roll was originally considered "bad", immoral, subversive music attributed to "bad boys". Characterized by instruments such as guitar, bass and drums, rock has given rise to many musical genres, which have made the success of many groups. Thus, The Beatles or The Rolling Stones have become two of the most popular British bands. At the end of the 1960s, rock became associated with politics and criticism of society. This is how punk rock was born in the 1970s. Since its creation and until today, rock has always had an important place in Western musical culture. Many contemporary artists practice it, merging it with other genres like electronic music.
